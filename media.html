An Excerpt from My Life's Journey

For the longest time, I battled with depression and a sense of low self-worth. I always saw myself through the lens of another's growth and progress. This robbed me of confidence and drive. I stopped aspiring for better because I couldn't see myself as good enough. I lost faith in my ability for greatness.

In a world where social media highlights the best moments of everyone’s life, it’s easy to fall into the trap of comparison. I saw curated snapshots of success, happiness, and perfection, and often felt inadequate or behind. However, this mindset was very detrimental to my mental well-being and personal growth. I had to relearn this crucial fact: my biggest competition should be myself, not others.

Comparing yourself to others is like comparing apples to oranges. Everyone’s path is different, shaped by unique experiences, challenges, and opportunities. When you measure your progress against someone else’s achievements, you’re not giving yourself a fair chance. The most dangerous fact about comparison is that it distracts you from your goals.

Why Prioritizing Your Growth is Healthy

The only person you should strive to be better than is the person you were yesterday. By focusing on self-improvement, you can achieve personal growth and fulfillment. Here’s why competing with yourself is the healthiest and most productive mindset:

  1. Personalized Progress: When you focus on your journey, you set goals that are meaningful and tailored to your strengths and interests. This personalized approach ensures that you’re growing in ways that matter to you.

  2. Celebrating Small Wins: Every step forward, no matter how small, is a victory. By recognizing and celebrating your progress, you build confidence and motivation to keep going.

  3. Learning from Setbacks: Competing with yourself allows you to view setbacks as learning opportunities. You become more resilient and adaptable, turning challenges into valuable experiences.

  4. Authentic Success: Success looks different for everyone. By defining your own standards and measuring success on your terms, you achieve authentic fulfillment and happiness.

Draw Inspiration and Wisdom

While it’s important not to compare yourself to others, it doesn’t mean you can’t draw inspiration from them. Learning from the stories and experiences of others can provide valuable insights and motivation.

  1. Seek Mentors, Not Idols: Find mentors who can guide and support you. Learn from their experiences without trying to replicate their journey exactly.

  2. Adapt, Don’t Adopt: Take what resonates with you from others’ stories and adapt it to fit your unique path. What works for someone else might need tweaking to work for you.

  3. Focus on Principles, Not Outcomes: Instead of fixating on the end results, focus on the principles and habits that led to others’ success. These can often be adapted to your circumstances.

Remember, no two people are the same, and the measure of success is subjective. Your journey is unique, and your competition is yourself. Strive to be better than you were yesterday, drawing inspiration and wisdom from others, but always keeping in mind that your path is your own. As we go along on this journey with AltSchool, remember your why—this is for you.
